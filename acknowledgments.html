Acknowledgments

I would like to begin by expressing my greatest gratitude to Dr. Samantha Seals, my thesis advisor, whom I met in the later parts of my academic journey at UWF. The period in which I was part of her Sampling Theory course also coincided with the mid-bachelor's career crisis I had been going through, where I began developing an interest in biostatistics but feared I was too far along my degree to change paths. Although the material covered in the course further reinforced my interest, a comment Dr. Seals left on my first class project gave me the confidence to say, "I want to be a biostatistician!" At the end of that semester, I asked Dr. Seals if I could be a part of her research, to which she responded with a strikingly enthusiastic and welcoming "Yes!" Thereafter, Dr. Seals became my academic, personal, and professional lodestar and continues to be to this day. Without Dr. Seals, neither would this research have been possible, nor would I have found to courage to pursue my passion, for which I am deeply grateful to her.

I would also like to take this opportunity to thank my family, without whom I might have been able to author a thesis (in a much shorter timespan) but not one that is worth writing. If it were not for my siblings, who frequently reminded me of the day-to-day joys I had come to overlook, my parents, who provided me the safety and stability I needed to recover and grow stronger after my failures, and my cat, who frequently reminded me it was time for a snack, I may have been able to complete a thesis but would not have become the person I am today, nor would I have learned the lessons I did along the way. For that, I am deeply appreciative of each and every one of them.
